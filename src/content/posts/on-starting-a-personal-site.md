There's something slightly absurd about a PhD student deciding, in the year 2026, that now is the time to build a personal website. The tools have existed for decades. The advice has been clear for just as long: put your work out there, build in public, write things down.

And yet here we are.

## Why now?

Partly it's practical. I wanted a place to collect notes on papers I've read, ideas I'm turning over, and the occasional longer piece of writing that doesn't fit neatly into a lab notebook or a Twitter thread. Partly it's vanity — I'll admit that freely.

But mostly it's because I've come to believe that **writing is thinking**, and thinking in public forces a kind of rigour that thinking in private doesn't. When you know someone might read it, you check your reasoning one more time.

## The setup

This site is built with [Astro](https://astro.build/), a static site generator that stays out of the way. Posts are written in Markdown. The whole thing is hosted on GitHub Pages. There is no JavaScript on the client unless I explicitly want it. That feels right.

I'm sure I'll fiddle with the design endlessly — that's half the fun — but the important thing is that the words come first.

## What to expect

Irregular updates. Notes on reinforcement learning, complexity, and whatever else catches my attention. Occasional book recommendations. No promises about frequency.

If any of it is useful to you, that's a happy accident.
